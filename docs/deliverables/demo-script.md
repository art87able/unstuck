# Demo Script

USER RECORDS

**0:00-0:10**
"If you have ADHD, task initiation can feel like staring at a wall. The task is not impossible, but your brain cannot find the first edge. And even when you do start, time-blindness means a 'quick five minutes' can quietly become twenty."

**0:10-0:25**
"This is Unstuck. I paste one overwhelming task — 'clean up my inbox and reply to the important emails' — pick a step size, and hit Enter. Instead of advice, I get tiny, timed steps. And notice: only the first step is lit up. No wall of tasks — just the next thing."

**0:25-0:40**
"I press Start on that step and just do it. When I hit Done, Unstuck measures the time for me — no self-tracking, which is exactly what a time-blind brain can't do. The estimate said 6 minutes; it took 14. The spotlight moves to the next step. This one doesn't matter anymore — Skip — and it's struck out without polluting my data."

**0:40-0:58**
"If a step is still too big, 'Still stuck?' breaks just that step into even tinier pieces, right in place. And as I log real times, watch the 'For you' estimates on the remaining steps shift — Unstuck is learning my personal time-blindness, per category. 'Your patterns' shows the receipts: my admin estimates run about 2× long, and it adjusts for that."

**0:58-1:10**
"When the last step resolves: 'Done — 5 steps in 48 minutes. The AI guessed 30 — you took 1.6× longer, and Unstuck now knows that.' It's not pretending I got faster. It's making the plan honest. I can copy the whole plan as a markdown checklist, and everything — my plan, my history — lives in my browser, not on a server."
